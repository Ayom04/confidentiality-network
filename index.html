Jane Doe: The Confidentiality Network

Jane Doe is a **survivor-centric toolkit** designed to build a truly better and safer campus life by transforming the way we address sexual assault, merging confidential support with cutting-edge AI.

**Team:** Jane Doe

The Problem: The Silence Barrier

This section clearly defines the urgent, three-part challenge students face, which we call the **'Silence Barrier.'** Sexual assault remains tragically pervasive on university campuses, yet the existing mechanisms for support and reporting are fundamentally broken, leading to massive underreporting. Understanding these deeply entrenched barriers is crucial to appreciating how Jane Doe provides a truly comprehensive, systemic solution.

1. Fear & Isolation

Lack of immediate, confidential, and non-judgmental support in the critical hours post-incident, leaving survivors feeling lost and alone during a traumatic time.

2. Evidence Paralysis

Confusion over what constitutes legally or administratively reportable evidence, leading to vital, time-sensitive information (time, location, communications) being lost or improperly documented.

3. Process Intimidation

The complex, opaque, and often re-victimizing nature of administrative or legal processes, which deters students from seeking justice and allows the cycle of underreporting to continue.

Why It Matters: Impact & Accountability

Our mission extends beyond a single campus‚Äîit's about driving institutional change. This section outlines the three key areas of impact: individual empowerment, data-driven accountability, and ensuring true inclusion for every female student, ultimately defining a safer campus culture.

Jane Doe converts the paralyzing confusion of the aftermath into clear, actionable steps, drastically lowering the barrier to reporting and seeking help.
