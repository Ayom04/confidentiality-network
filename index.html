A Universal Shield for Campus Safety

Jane Doe is a survivor-centric toolkit designed to transform the way university campuses address sexual assault and all forms of gender-based violence (GBV) , merging confidential support with cutting-edge, localized AI.

Team: Jane Doe | Theme: Building Better Campus Life

1. The Problem: The Silence Barrier

Sexual assault and rape remain tragically pervasive issues on university campuses, yet the mechanisms for support and reporting are fundamentally broken. The current system fails survivors; it prioritizes institutional protection or police procedure over the student‚Äôs well-being and right to justice.

Fear & Isolation

Lack of immediate, confidential, and non-judgmental support in the critical hours post-incident.

Evidence Paralysis

Confusion over what constitutes reportable evidence, leading to vital information (time, location, communications) being lost or improperly documented.

Process Intimidation

The complex, opaque, and often re-victimizing administrative or legal processes, resulting in massive underreporting, amplified for disabled or non-native English speakers.

2. The Solution: Jane Doe - The Survivor-Centric Toolkit

Jane Doe is a Progressive Web Application (PWA) , chosen for its cross-platform availability and quick installation, ensuring it is a confidential, secure, and accessible Digital Triage and Documentation Platform. The application's architecture is built on five core pillars of support.

    3. Why It Matters: Impact and Accountability

    Jane Doe doesn't just treat the symptom; it addresses systemic failure by focusing on survivor empowerment and institutional accountability, creating a truly safe campus for everyone.

    Empowerment through Information

    The AI converts the paralyzing confusion of the aftermath into clear, actionable steps, drastically lowering the barrier to reporting and seeking help.

    Data-Driven Accountability

    By standardizing and securing evidence collection, it provides administrators and legal teams with cleaner, more complete cases, making it harder for institutions to ignore or mishandle reports.

    Inclusion for All

    By prioritizing disability, language access, and proxy reporting, Jane Doe ensures that all female students, regardless of background or ability, have a confidential path to justice.
